at first glance , parts of alberta have excellent geothermal heating potential . however , a complicating factor is the often wildly fluctuating ambient temperatures due to chinook winds , where temperatures can go from minus 20 ° c to plus 20 ° c in the space of a few hours . the response time of geothermal heating systems can be slow to keep up with this instability ; likewise , the electrical energy required to pump the geothermal heat can be very expensive .
